Craig Sweigart

Understanding what many men struggle to see

Many women hesitate before working with a male relationship coach — and that hesitation makes complete sense. Relationship coaching requires trust, vulnerability, and the confidence that the person across from you genuinely understands your experience.

Craig brings something uncommon to this work: a genuine ability to understand and hold space for women's perspectives in ways that many men simply cannot. That's not a claim made lightly — it's a capacity built through decades of meaningful relationships, honest reflection, and a deep commitment to understanding the full human experience of partnership.

With a background in communications, philosophy, and psychology, Craig approaches relationship coaching not as an outside expert dispensing advice, but as a thoughtful guide helping couples and individuals see themselves and each other more clearly — often for the first time in years.

Table Rock Wellness is based in Boise, Idaho — named for the iconic landmark that has stood watch over this valley for centuries. Like the rock itself, the work here is grounded, enduring, and built on something solid.

Most couples in crisis aren't failing because they've stopped loving each other. They're failing because they've stopped truly seeing each other. — Craig Sweigart, Table Rock Wellness

The Approach

What guides the work at Table Rock Wellness

01

Self-Awareness First

Real change begins when each person understands why they show up the way they do — the patterns, the triggers, the defaults under pressure. This isn't blame. It's clarity.

02

Empathy as a Skill

Empathy isn't something you either have or you don't. It's a practice — one that can be developed, deepened, and brought to bear even in the hardest conversations.

03

Active Re-Engagement

The measure of success isn't staying together — it's choosing each other with intention. Clients leave Table Rock Wellness with renewed energy to actively invest in their relationship.

A note about coaching vs. therapy

Table Rock Wellness provides relationship coaching — a forward-focused, goal-oriented service designed to help you identify patterns, build self-awareness, and develop practical strategies for your relationship. Coaching is not a licensed mental health service and is not a substitute for therapy, counseling, or psychiatric care. If you are experiencing a mental health condition, Craig will always be honest with you about whether coaching or professional clinical support is the right fit.
